**Case Report: The Lantern Room Death**

At 9:40 p.m. on 14 October, Dr. Mara Venn, founder of the private Halewick Oceanic Archive, was found dead inside the locked Lantern Room of Grayhook Observatory, a converted signal tower on the Northumberland coast. The apparent cause was cardiac failure after drinking medicated tea. The real cause was poisoning by aconitine delivered through a doctored glass stirring rod. The culprit was **Silas Wren**, the archive’s quiet acquisitions manager, who believed Mara had discovered his years-long theft and forgery scheme.

The case involved five principal suspects: Silas Wren; Elian Voss, Mara’s estranged nephew; Cora Bell, the night curator; Father Ivo Hale, a local historian; and Lenna Quist, Mara’s former business partner. All had access to the observatory that evening, all had reason to fear Mara’s midnight announcement, and each saw only part of the truth.

Mara had summoned them for a 10:00 p.m. meeting, saying she would reveal “the one person who has been feeding on the dead.” She had recently learned that rare shipwreck letters in the archive were being replaced with convincing forgeries and sold privately. She had not yet named the thief aloud, but she had hidden proof inside a lantern-slide box.

The evening began at 6:15 p.m., when Silas arrived early, claiming he needed to catalogue storm-damaged maps. At 6:40 he entered the Lantern Room alone and replaced Mara’s usual glass stirring rod with one he had coated in soluble aconitine resin, knowing she always stirred her valerian tea clockwise before drinking. At 7:05, Cora saw him leaving the room carrying nothing, but missed that his left glove was inside out from handling the poisoned rod.

At 7:30, Lenna arrived and argued with Mara in the map gallery over an old breach of contract. Elian came at 8:05, drunk and angry about being cut out of Mara’s will. Father Ivo entered at 8:20 with parish records Mara had requested. At 8:45, Mara privately told Cora that “the thief is not the one who shouts,” which Cora later misunderstood as clearing Elian.

At 9:05, Silas cut power to the south corridor for three minutes under the pretense of checking storm shutters. During that blackout he removed the lantern-slide box from Mara’s desk, but missed the fact that Mara had already transferred the key evidence: a torn watermark strip, bearing his supplier’s mark, into her tide-clock.

At 9:18, Mara entered the Lantern Room and locked the door from within to prepare her notes. At 9:24, Cora brought her tea and left it outside the pass-through hatch. Mara pulled it in herself. At 9:31, Father Ivo heard a ceramic spoon fall, but did not know Mara never used spoons. At 9:36, Elian heard Mara gasp, “The rod,” but assumed she meant the lightning conductor outside. At 9:40, Lenna forced the door with an iron fire hook after seeing Mara collapse through the glass panel.

The physical evidence was decisive. The cup contained only harmless valerian and honey. The spoon had no poison. The glass stirring rod, found rinsed but still faintly tacky at one end, held aconitine traces. Silas had rinsed it after entering with the others, pretending to steady himself near the washbasin. His glove later showed the same resin. The stolen lantern-slide box proved consciousness of guilt, but the hidden watermark strip proved motive: it matched forged documents Silas had sold through an intermediary.

Each witness held a fragment. Cora knew Mara trusted the tea routine but missed the glove. Elian heard the dying clue but misread it. Ivo heard the wrong utensil fall, proving someone had planted or disturbed a spoon. Lenna saw the collapse but wrongly suspected Elian because of the argument. Only Silas knew the rod mattered, which is why he tried to clean it.

The information asymmetry made the case solvable. No witness saw the poisoning itself, but several had opportunities to observe adjacent facts: Cora saw Silas leave the Lantern Room early; Ivo heard the false spoon clue; Elian heard Mara identify the object; Lenna saw the locked-room condition; and Mara’s tide-clock preserved the motive evidence Silas failed to find.

The final reconstruction is that Silas poisoned the stirring rod before Mara entered, staged a document theft to misdirect suspicion toward inheritance and professional rivalry, then exploited the locked room to make the death seem self-contained. Mara locked herself in alive, unknowingly activated the poison by stirring her tea, and died before naming him clearly. The conclusion that Silas Wren killed Dr. Mara Venn is held with high confidence: motive, means, prior access, post-crime behavior, physical poison transfer, and the victim’s final words all converge on him.
