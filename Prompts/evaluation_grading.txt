Input: <crime investigation story>

Task: Analyze the crime investigation story and return a structured JSON object.

Instructions:
1. Identify all the people in the case, their relations, and their contradicting or complementary stories.
2. Apply NATO Intelligence Grading to each person as an evidentiary source. Assess source reliability separately from information credibility. Do not force the two grades to match.

Definitions:
- Source reliability = trustworthiness of the person as a source: firsthand access, competence, consistency, bias, deception, hearsay, and uncertainty. A = completely reliable; B = usually reliable; C = fairly reliable; D = not usually reliable; E = unreliable; F = cannot be judged.
- Information credibility = factual accuracy of the source's material claims against the final resolved outcome: culprit, method, timeline, motive, and key evidence. Grade only material claims; use the lowest justified score among them. 1 = confirmed; 2 = probably true; 3 = possibly true; 4 = doubtful; 5 = contradicted/improbable; 6 = cannot be judged.

Output JSON only:
{
  "witnesses": [
    {
      "id": "<witness id>",
      "name": "<witness full name>",
      "statement": "<witness claims>",
      "source_reliability": "<A|B|C|D|E|F>",
      "information_credibility": "<1|2|3|4|5|6>"
    }
  ]
}
